Common Causes of Infertility

  • PCOS and ovulation disorders
  • Blocked or damaged fallopian tubes
  • Endometriosis
  • Male-factor infertility
  • Hormonal imbalance or thyroid issues
  • Age-related decline in fertility
  • Unexplained infertility

Fertility Evaluation & Treatment Options

  • Complete fertility assessment for both partners
  • Ovulation tracking and hormonal testing
  • Ultrasound and tubal assessment
  • Lifestyle and pre-conception guidance
  • Ovulation-induction medication and IUI
  • IVF counselling and referral pathway

Frequently Asked Questions

When should we see a fertility specialist?

Generally after 12 months of trying (or 6 months if you're over 35) — though it's worth coming in sooner if you already know of a risk factor.

Does treatment always mean IVF?

Not at all — many couples conceive with much simpler treatments first. IVF is only ever recommended when it's truly needed.

Can PCOS-related infertility be treated?

Yes — ovulation support helps many women with PCOS go on to conceive.

Is the male partner evaluated too?

Yes, always — fertility evaluation looks at both partners, not just one.
