When Is a Pregnancy Considered High-Risk?

Your pregnancy may be classed as high-risk if any of these apply:

  • Maternal age under 18 or over 35
  • Pre-existing diabetes, high blood pressure or thyroid disorders
  • Twin or multiple pregnancy
  • Previous complications, recurrent miscarriage or preterm birth
  • Pre-eclampsia or gestational diabetes
  • Conception through IVF or other fertility treatment
  • Heart, kidney or autoimmune conditions

Warning Signs to Never Ignore in Pregnancy

Contact your doctor promptly if you notice:

  • Severe headache or blurred vision
  • Heavy swelling of the face or hands
  • A noticeable drop in your baby's movements
  • Vaginal bleeding or severe abdominal pain
  • Persistent vomiting or high fever

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a high-risk pregnancy have a normal delivery?

Often yes — with close monitoring many high-risk pregnancies proceed to a safe vaginal birth, decided individually.

How often will I need check-ups?

More frequently than a routine pregnancy; scans and reviews are scheduled to your specific risk factors.

Is a high-risk pregnancy dangerous?

"High-risk" means extra care is needed, not that a problem is certain; expert monitoring greatly improves outcomes.

Which doctor is best for high-risk pregnancy in Lucknow?

Can I plan my pregnancy if I have a medical condition?
