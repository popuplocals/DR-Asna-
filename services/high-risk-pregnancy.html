When is a pregnancy considered high-risk?

Your pregnancy may be classed as high-risk if any of the following apply:

  • Maternal age below 18 or above 35
  • Pre-existing conditions such as diabetes, high blood pressure or thyroid disorders
  • Twin or multiple pregnancy
  • Previous pregnancy complications, recurrent miscarriage or preterm birth
  • Pregnancy-related conditions like pre-eclampsia or gestational diabetes
  • Conception through IVF or other fertility treatment
