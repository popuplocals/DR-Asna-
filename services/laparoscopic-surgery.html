Laparoscopic surgery uses small keyhole incisions and a camera-guided instrument instead of one large open cut. As an experienced laparoscopic surgeon in Lucknow, Dr. Asna Zehra Naqvi offers advanced minimally invasive procedures that mean less pain, minimal scarring and a quicker return to daily life.

Conditions Treated Laparoscopically

  • Ovarian cysts and benign ovarian tumours
  • Uterine fibroids (myomectomy)
  • Endometriosis
  • Ectopic pregnancy
  • Adhesions and pelvic pain
  • Hysterectomy (removal of the uterus) where indicated

Why Choose Minimally Invasive Surgery

  • Smaller incisions and minimal scarring
  • Less post-operative pain
  • Shorter hospital stay
  • Faster recovery and return to activities
  • Lower risk of wound infection

What to Expect — Before, During & After

Before surgery you receive a clear explanation of the procedure, anaesthesia and recovery. The operation is performed through a few small incisions under general anaesthesia. Afterwards, most women have only mild discomfort, managed with medication, and return home quickly with simple aftercare instructions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is laparoscopic surgery painful?

Far less than open surgery thanks to small incisions; mild discomfort is managed with medication.

When can I return to work?

Many women resume light activity within a week, depending on the procedure.

Will it leave scars?

Only tiny scars from the keyhole incisions, which usually fade.

Is keyhole surgery suitable for fibroids?

Yes — many fibroids can be removed laparoscopically (myomectomy); suitability is assessed individually.
