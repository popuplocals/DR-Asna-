Signs You May Have PCOS

  • Irregular, delayed or missed periods
  • Weight gain or difficulty losing weight
  • Excess facial or body hair (hirsutism)
  • Acne or oily skin
  • Hair thinning on the scalp
  • Difficulty conceiving

What Causes Irregular Periods?

Irregular cycles can stem from PCOS, thyroid problems, stress, sudden weight change, excessive exercise, or perimenopause. The first step is finding the cause — which guides the right treatment.

How PCOS & Irregular Periods Are Managed

  • Accurate diagnosis with ultrasound and hormone tests
  • A personalised plan combining lifestyle, diet and medication
  • Cycle regulation and symptom control
  • Weight, insulin and skin/hair-symptom management
  • Fertility support where pregnancy is the goal
  • Long-term monitoring to lower risks like type-2 diabetes

Frequently Asked Questions

Can PCOS be cured?

There isn't really a "cure" — but with the right plan, symptoms and cycles can be managed very well, often for good.

Can I get pregnant with PCOS?

Yes — many women with PCOS go on to conceive, often with fairly simple ovulation support.

Is PCOS the same as PCOD?

They overlap quite a bit — both involve an ovarian or hormonal imbalance, and both are managed similarly once properly diagnosed.

Does PCOS need lifelong treatment?

Management tends to be ongoing, but it adapts as things change — and lifestyle adjustments really do help along the way.
