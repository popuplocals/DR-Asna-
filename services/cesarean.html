When a vaginal birth carries risk for the mother or baby, a cesarean section is a safe, well-established way to deliver. Dr. Asna Zehra Naqvi performs both planned and emergency C-sections in Lucknow with meticulous surgical care and full support before, during and after birth.

When a C-Section May Be Advised

  • Baby in breech or transverse position
  • Placenta previa or other placental concerns
  • Labour not progressing safely
  • Fetal distress requiring urgent delivery
  • Certain maternal conditions or previous C-sections

Planned vs Emergency C-Section

A planned (elective) C-section is scheduled in advance when a vaginal birth is known to carry risk, allowing calm preparation. An emergency C-section is performed when concerns arise during labour. Dr. Naqvi prepares you for both possibilities so there are no surprises on the day.

Recovery After a C-Section

Most mothers are up and moving within a day and home within a few days. Full recovery takes a few weeks; Dr. Naqvi guides you on pain relief, wound care, activity and when to seek help — so healing is smooth and safe.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long is recovery after a C-section?

Home within a few days; full recovery over a few weeks with guided aftercare.

Is a planned C-section safer than an emergency one?

A planned C-section allows preparation and is generally calmer; the right choice is decided with you.

Can I have a normal delivery after a C-section?

Often yes (VBAC) — assessed individually.

Will a C-section affect future pregnancies?

Usually future pregnancies are managed safely with appropriate monitoring.
