Many ovarian cysts are harmless and resolve on their own — but some need careful monitoring or treatment. Dr. Asna Zehra Naqvi provides accurate diagnosis and the right management for ovarian cysts in Lucknow, from watchful waiting to advanced keyhole surgery when required.

Symptoms to Watch For

  • Lower abdominal or pelvic pain
  • Bloating or a feeling of fullness
  • Irregular periods
  • Pain during intercourse
  • Frequent urination
  • Sudden, severe pain (needs urgent care)

Types of Ovarian Cysts

Most are functional cysts linked to the menstrual cycle and clear up naturally. Others — such as dermoid cysts, endometriomas (chocolate cysts) and cystadenomas — may need treatment. Diagnosis identifies the type so management is appropriate.

How Ovarian Cysts Are Treated

  • Ultrasound and assessment to understand the cyst type and size
  • Monitoring for simple cysts that often resolve naturally
  • Medication where appropriate
  • Minimally invasive laparoscopic removal for larger, persistent or symptomatic cysts

Frequently Asked Questions

Are ovarian cysts dangerous?

Most are benign and harmless; assessment determines whether monitoring or treatment is needed.

Will surgery affect fertility?

Laparoscopic removal is designed to preserve healthy ovarian tissue and fertility wherever possible.

Do all cysts need surgery?

No — many resolve on their own and only need monitoring.

Can ovarian cysts come back?

Some types can recur; follow-up helps catch them early.
